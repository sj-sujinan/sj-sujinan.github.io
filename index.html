About Me

I am a neuro-AI researcher with a multidisciplinary background, working at the intersection of human cognition, adaptive learning, and machine intelligence. My research, which spans both theoretical and applied domains, focuses on how we can design AI systems that not only learn from data but also actively collaborate with and learn from people.

My work focuses on developing computational models and experimental systems that explore the link between human cognition and AI. I am particularly interested in how we can use insights from neuroscience to build more intelligent and human-centric systems. My research is deeply interdisciplinary, combining computational rigor with empirical experimentation across behavioral science, neuroscience, and machine learning.

I am always open to collaboration and welcome inquiries.

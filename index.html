About Me

I'm a neuro-AI researcher working at the intersection of human cognition, adaptive learning, and machine intelligence. My research bridges cognitive science, computational neuroscience, and artificial intelligence — with a particular focus on how we can design AI systems that learn not only from data, but from people.

Trained across disciplines, I develop closed-loop experimental systems and cognitive models that adapt to human behaviour in real time. From human-in-the-loop reinforcement learning frameworks like the Deep Neural Experimenter (DNE), to metacognitive signal processing pipelines such as MetaDot, my work spans both theoretical and applied domains.

A key focus of my research is on building interpretable and adaptive experimental designs — systems that don’t just observe human learning, but actively optimise it. I regularly integrate tools from Bayesian modelling, signal detection theory, GLM / mixed-effects modelling, and deep learning to uncover how reasoning emerges from the interaction between environment, strategy, and internal uncertainty.

I believe that neuro-AI research must be deeply interdisciplinary — combining computational rigour with cognitive insight, and empirical experimentation with algorithmic innovation. My projects often involve iterative collaborations across behavioural science, neuroscience, and machine learning to push forward how we understand — and design — intelligent systems.
